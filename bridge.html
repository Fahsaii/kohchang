RED BRIDGE

It is also known as the Baan Salak Phet Mangrove Forest Restoration Project. It is a nature study route on a red wooden bridge that stretches to the end at the mouth of Salak Phet Bay, a distance of 520 meters. We will see a variety of strange-looking plants that allow us to recognize that. The mangrove forest is a rich source of food for many animals. Some parts of the corridor will have trees leaning together like a tree tunnel. Walk to the middle of the bridge to see the picture. The red bridge contrasts with the green mangrove leaves alternating the horizon.
